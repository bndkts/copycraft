# brand-brief.md — fill-in template

Copy everything between the rules below into the user's `.claude/brand-brief.md` and fill
it in during the interview. Replace every `‹placeholder›`. Leave a visible `TODO:` on any
section the user skipped — never invent positioning, proof, or audience.

A worked example (the **nebty** brand — brand protection / B2B security, **Sie**, warm)
follows the blank template, clearly labeled. It is **constructed for illustration**, not
real customer copy. Use it as a model for the level of specificity to aim for.

---

## TEMPLATE (copy this)

```markdown
# Brand Brief — ‹Brand name›

> The single source of truth for this brand's copy. Every copycraft skill reads this file.
> Last updated: ‹YYYY-MM-DD›. Primary language(s): ‹EN / DE / both›.

## 1. One-liner
‹What it is, for whom, in one plain sentence. No adjectives, no hype.›

## 2. ICP & buyer
- **Reader / user role(s):** ‹who actually reads the copy›
- **Economic buyer (if different):** ‹who signs off›
- **Their top fears:** ‹what keeps them up — what they're afraid of getting wrong›
- **Their top wants:** ‹the outcome they're hired to deliver›

## 3. Positioning & primary value prop
- **Positioning:** ‹what this brand does better/differently — must pass the competitor
  copy-paste test: a competitor could NOT paste this onto their site truthfully›
- **Primary value prop (the one outcome):** ‹the single result the reader gets›

## 4. Voice
- **Voice adjectives (3–5, constant):** ‹e.g. clear, calm, precise, confident›
- **Anti-adjectives (1–2, never this):** ‹e.g. hypey, jargon-heavy›
- **Tone shifts by context:** ‹how the voice flexes: error page = ‹…›, launch = ‹…›,
  sales = ‹…›. Voice stays; tone moves.›

## 5. Anrede decision (German) — BINDING, stated once
- **Choice:** ‹du OR Sie› — chosen because ‹audience + channel reason›.
- **Register:** ‹e.g. modern, warm; no Amtsdeutsch›
- **Rule:** never mix du/Sie within a journey. Every surface (hero, forms, tooltips,
  emails, errors) uses the same Anrede.
- **Verb forms to use** (fill the chosen column; delete the other):

  | Function | du | Sie |
  |---|---|---|
  | test/try | Teste / Probier aus | Testen Sie / Probieren Sie aus |
  | get it | Hol dir | Holen Sie sich |
  | start | Starte / Leg los | Starten Sie / Legen Sie los |
  | secure it | Sichere dir | Sichern Sie sich |
  | learn | Erfahre | Erfahren Sie |
  | protect | Schütze | Schützen Sie |
  | plan/book | Plane / Buche | Planen Sie / Buchen Sie |
  | do | Mach | Machen Sie |
  | possessive | Dein / Deine | Ihr / Ihre |
  | object pronoun | dich | Sie |
  | dative pronoun | dir | Ihnen |

## 6. Proof points / trust signals
‹Checkable facts only — these replace adjectives like "leading" and "trusted".›
- ‹e.g. GDPR-compliant; ISO-certified›
- ‹e.g. data hosted in Germany›
- ‹e.g. response within 24 hours›
- ‹e.g. 1,200+ customers / named clients›
- ‹e.g. transparent pricing; fixed point of contact; cancel anytime›

> Trust-vocabulary menu to draw from (only keep what's TRUE): DSGVO-konform / GDPR-compliant,
> ISO-zertifiziert, Serverstandort Deutschland, SSL-verschlüsselt, in ‹N› Wochen einsatzbereit,
> über ‹N› Kunden, Antwort in 24 Stunden, fester Ansprechpartner, transparente Preise,
> Made in Germany, geprüft, nachweislich, revisionssicher.

## 7. Terminology glossary (bilingual)
‹How this brand spells its words and which English terms stay English in German copy.›

| Term | Stays English? | German word | Canonical spelling | EN ↔ DE equivalent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ‹Dashboard› | ‹yes› | ‹—› | ‹das Dashboard› | ‹dashboard ↔ Dashboard› |
| ‹Meeting› | ‹no› | ‹Sitzung› | ‹die Sitzung› | ‹meeting ↔ Sitzung› |
| ‹…› | ‹…› | ‹…› | ‹…› | ‹…› |

## 8. Banned words & phrases
‹Each banned item gets a replacement instruction — a fact, number, or mechanism — so copy
improves, not just shrinks. Keep two buckets separate.›

**Filler / Floskeln (safe to ban — say nothing a competitor couldn't say):**
innovativ, ganzheitlich, maßgeschneidert, kundenorientiert, einzigartig, nachhaltig,
hochwertig, zukunftssicher, erstklassig, führend, leistungsstark, revolutionär, disruptiv;
„maßgeschneiderte Lösungen“, „Lösungen aus einer Hand“, „Ihr starker/verlässlicher Partner“,
„Wir setzen Maßstäbe“, „Erleben Sie den Unterschied“; world-class, cutting-edge, best-in-class,
game-changing, seamless, robust, leverage. ‹+ add the brand's own pet peeves›

**Brand preferences (NOT errors — recorded because the brand dislikes them):**
‹e.g. "Sinn machen" → use "ergibt Sinn" (note: contested; Eisenberg defends "Sinn machen" —
this is a house-style choice, not a correctness rule)›

## 9. Voice samples (Stimmproben) — golden copy
‹3–5 real, **approved** lines of on-brand copy, each labeled with its surface. These are
the strongest voice control in this brief: adjectives describe the voice, samples
demonstrate it. Skills match the samples' sentence length, rhythm, and word choice —
when a sample and an adjective pull apart, the sample wins.›

- **Hero:** ‹an approved hero line›
- **Feature blurb:** ‹an approved short paragraph›
- **Email opener:** ‹an approved first sentence of a customer email›
- **CTA + friction-reducer:** ‹an approved button + the line beside it›

**Never like this (one counter-example):**
> ‹one sentence that is exactly what the brand must NOT sound like — ideally a real,
> rejected draft›

Why it's off: ‹one line — name the tells, e.g. "Floskel stack, no fact, any competitor
could say it"›
```

## EXAMPLE — filled brief (illustrative, constructed; NOT real customer copy)

```markdown
# Brand Brief — nebty

> The single source of truth for this brand's copy. Every copycraft skill reads this file.
> Last updated: 2026-06-29. Primary language(s): both (de-DE primary, EN secondary).

## 1. One-liner
nebty finds the fake websites, phishing pages, and impostor profiles abusing a company's
brand and gets them taken down.

## 2. ICP & buyer
- **Reader / user role(s):** brand-protection, security, and compliance teams at mid-size
  and enterprise companies in DACH.
- **Economic buyer (if different):** CISO / Head of Brand or Legal who signs the contract.
- **Their top fears:** customers getting scammed by a fake shop using their logo; finding
  out late, from an angry customer or the press; a takedown that drags on for weeks.
- **Their top wants:** know within the hour, prove they acted, get the fake offline fast.

## 3. Positioning & primary value prop
- **Positioning:** not just monitoring — detection plus the actual takedown, with proof of
  removal. (A competitor selling only alerts could not paste this truthfully.)
- **Primary value prop:** you hear about an impersonation the moment it goes live, and we
  confirm when it's gone.

## 4. Voice
- **Voice adjectives:** clear, calm, precise, quietly confident.
- **Anti-adjectives:** hypey, alarmist, jargon-heavy.
- **Tone shifts by context:** incident alert = factual and brief; landing page = reassuring
  and concrete; sales follow-up = warm. Voice stays calm and precise throughout.

## 5. Anrede decision (German) — BINDING, stated once
- **Choice:** Sie — chosen because the buyers are security/compliance teams where a wrong
  *du* reads as careless and a *Sie* costs nothing.
- **Register:** modern and warm; no Amtsdeutsch.
- **Rule:** never mix du/Sie within a journey. Hero, forms, tooltips, emails, errors all siezen.
- **Verb forms to use:**

  | Function | Sie |
  |---|---|
  | test/try | Testen Sie / Probieren Sie aus |
  | get it | Holen Sie sich |
  | start | Starten Sie / Legen Sie los |
  | secure it | Sichern Sie sich |
  | learn | Erfahren Sie |
  | protect | Schützen Sie |
  | possessive | Ihr / Ihre |
  | object/dative pronoun | Sie / Ihnen |

## 6. Proof points / trust signals
- DSGVO-konform; Serverstandort Deutschland.
- Antwort in 24 Stunden; fester Ansprechpartner.
- 1.200+ Marken geschützt.
- Takedown im Schnitt in unter 48 Stunden bestätigt.
- Transparente Preise; monatlich kündbar.

## 7. Terminology glossary (bilingual)

| Term | Stays English? | German word | Canonical spelling | EN ↔ DE equivalent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Takedown | yes | — | der Takedown | takedown ↔ Takedown |
| Phishing | yes | — | das Phishing | phishing ↔ Phishing |
| Fake shop | yes (loan) | — | der Fake-Shop | fake shop ↔ Fake-Shop |
| Dashboard | yes | — | das Dashboard | dashboard ↔ Dashboard |
| Brand protection | no | Markenschutz | der Markenschutz | brand protection ↔ Markenschutz |
| Monitoring | yes | (Überwachung) | das Monitoring | monitoring ↔ Monitoring |

## 8. Banned words & phrases
**Filler / Floskeln (banned):** innovativ, ganzheitlich, maßgeschneidert, „Lösungen aus
einer Hand“, „Ihr verlässlicher Partner“, world-class, cutting-edge, seamless, game-changing.
→ Replace with a number, a named threat, or the takedown time.

**Brand preferences (not errors):** avoid „am Ende des Tages“ (calque) → use „unterm Strich“;
prefer „ergibt Sinn“ over „Sinn machen“ (house style; the latter is contested, not wrong).

## 9. Voice samples (Stimmproben) — golden copy

- **Hero:** „Gefälschte Shops mit Ihrem Logo? Sie erfahren es zuerst – und wir nehmen
  sie vom Netz.“
- **Feature blurb:** „Wir überwachen über 200 Plattformen rund um die Uhr. Bei einem
  Treffer melden wir die Seite innerhalb von 24 Stunden zur Löschung – und Sie sehen
  jeden Schritt im Dashboard.“
- **Email opener:** „Kurzes Update zu Ihrer Marke: drei neue Funde diese Woche, zwei
  davon sind bereits offline.“
- **CTA + friction-reducer:** „Markenschutz starten“ · „Keine Kreditkarte nötig.“

**Never like this (counter-example):**
> „Als Ihr verlässlicher Partner für ganzheitlichen Markenschutz setzen wir Maßstäbe in
> der digitalen Sicherheit.“

Why it's off: Floskel stack (verlässlicher Partner, ganzheitlich, Maßstäbe setzen), no
fact, no reader — any competitor could paste it unchanged.
```
