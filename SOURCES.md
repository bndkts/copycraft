# Sources & Attribution

copycraft's rules are reimplemented in our own words from the authorities below. Where a
rule is contestable, the skill body cites the source inline. We bundle no third-party
skill text; we link to upstream and credit the craft.

## Honesty rule (hard rules vs. contested style)

We separate two kinds of guidance, and so should you when reading the skills:

- **Hard rules** — genuine standard-German errors: `in 2024` ✗ (use `2024`/`im Jahr 2024`);
  the Deppenleerzeichen ✗ (`Social Media Plattform` → `Social-Media-Plattform`); straight
  or English quotation marks ✗ (use `„…“`); glued/!mis-ordered currency ✗ (`1,99€` →
  `1,99 €`, `1.000,00 €`).
- **Contested style** — "smells" to downgrade, not errors to forbid: `Sinn machen`
  (linguist **Peter Eisenberg** argues it is not provably an anglicism; Duden lists it as
  *umgangssprachlich*), `am Ende des Tages`, `nicht wirklich`, `es braucht`, `die Extrameile`.

## Authoritative German references

- **Duden** — grammar, spelling, quotation marks, compounds, currency/number conventions.
- **Schweizerische Bundeskanzlei**, *Empfehlungen für den Umgang mit Anglizismen* (2020) —
  the three-question anglicism test; meaning-loans (`realisieren`, `der Elefant im Raum`).
- **DIN 5008** — business writing standard: numbers, dates, currency, dashes.
- **LanguageTool** (+ LanguageTool Insights) — calques, typography, anglicisms.
- **Scribbr.de** — compounds, capitalization of English nouns, Nominalstil vs. Verbalstil.
- **Universität Hohenheim — Hohenheimer Verständlichkeitsindex (HIX)**,
  Prof. Dr. Frank Brettschneider — comprehensibility / sentence-length research.
- **dpa** — readability benchmark (~9 words/sentence) widely cited in German style guidance.
- **typefacts.com** — German quotation-mark mnemonic ("99 unten, 66 oben").

## du / Sie data (directional, not fresh-2026 SaaS-specific)

- **Appinio** (April 2019, n≈4,877) — 61% read Siezen as respect; only 8% want it abolished.
- **Sirius Campus / Aeiforia**, *Kundenmonitor Assekuranz 2022* — 53% prefer "Sie" in
  advisory contexts vs. 17% "du" (trust-sensitive B2B proxy).
- **benutzerfreun.de** reader poll (2021) — 84% are fine being gesiezt.
- Brand verdicts (June 2026) from live German pages / quoted copy: du — sevDesk,
  Lexware Office, awork, Notion, Slack, Trade Republic, N26; Sie — Personio, HubSpot DE,
  Pipedrive DE, DeepL. Re-verify before relying on any single one.

## Caveats

- du/Sie figures are directional (2019–2022 studies); let audience judgment govern.
- "Long sentences / nominalization = translationese" is a **clarity** heuristic; the
  sourced translationese tells are calqued idioms + compound/typography errors.
- All nebty example sentences are **constructed**; have a native copywriter review before
  publishing.
- Ecosystem facts (star counts, repo contents) are point-in-time (mid-2026).
- Targets **de-DE**. Austria/Switzerland differ (currency placement, `ss`/`ß`, `«…»`).
