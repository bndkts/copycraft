# Before / After — English (constructed examples)

> **Note:** All examples are **original and constructed** for illustration. They are
> **not** copied from nebty's site or any competitor. Sections 1–6: brand protection /
> anti-phishing (B2B security). Section 7 shows a second domain so the patterns don't
> stick to one industry. These show the polishing principles in
> `shared/references/conversion-principles.md` and the de-AI rules in
> `skills/humanizer-en/`.

Each pair shows ✗ weak → ✓ stronger, plus *why*.

---

## 1. Hero

**✗ Before**
> "Our innovative, end-to-end platform empowers organizations to holistically
> safeguard their brand integrity in today's fast-paced digital landscape."

**✓ After**
> "We find the fake websites, phishing pages, and impostor profiles abusing your
> brand — and get them taken down."

**Why:** Cut the AI/corporate tells ("innovative," "holistically," "in today's
fast-paced … landscape," "empowers"). Concrete verbs and concrete threats. The reader's
outcome leads; the company recedes.

---

## 2. Value proposition

**✗ Before**
> "Leveraging cutting-edge technology, we provide best-in-class solutions to address
> a wide range of brand security challenges."

**✓ After**
> "You hear about a fake shop the moment it goes live — not weeks later from an angry
> customer. We detect it, report it, and confirm when it's gone."

**Why:** "Leveraging," "cutting-edge," "best-in-class," "solutions," "a wide range of"
say nothing — a competitor could paste them verbatim. Replace with the specific moment
of value and the mechanism (detect → report → confirm).

---

## 3. Feature description

**✗ Before**
> "Our platform offers automated, AI-powered monitoring capabilities across social
> media channels to facilitate the identification of impersonation attempts."

**✓ After**
> "We watch social media around the clock and flag impostor accounts automatically —
> usually within the hour."

**Why:** "offers … capabilities … to facilitate the identification of" is wind-up.
Lead with the verb, add a concrete time-to-value. "AI-powered" earns its place only if
the speed is the proof — so show the speed instead.

---

## 4. Trust line

**✗ Before**
> "We are a trusted, reliable partner committed to delivering world-class security
> for businesses of all sizes."

**✓ After**
> "GDPR-compliant, data hosted in Germany, incident response within 24 hours,
> 1,200+ brands protected."

**Why:** "trusted, reliable partner … world-class … of all sizes" is the English twin
of German Floskeln — pure filler. Swap claims for checkable facts; proof is what makes
trust believable.

---

## 5. CTA

**✗ Before**: "Learn more" · "Submit" · "Click here"
**✓ After**: "Start protecting your brand" · "Book a 20-minute demo" · "Scan my brand now"

**Why:** Action verb + benefit, one primary action, no generic "Learn more." The reader
should know exactly what happens next and what they get.

---

## 6. AI-tells removed (humanizer pass)

| ✗ Tell | ✓ Fix |
|---|---|
| "It's not just monitoring — it's peace of mind." | (cut the negative parallelism; state the benefit plainly) |
| em dash everywhere — like this — and this — | use sparingly; vary with periods and commas |
| "delve into," "navigate the landscape," "robust suite" | "look at," "handle," "tools that work" |
| "fast, simple, and powerful" (rule of three on autopilot) | pick the one that's true and prove it |
| "Studies show this matters." | name the study, the number, or cut the claim |

## 7. A second domain (salon booking software)

Same rules, different industry — the patterns hold anywhere.

**✗ Before (hero)**
> "Our innovative booking platform empowers salons of all sizes to seamlessly manage
> their appointments in today's fast-paced beauty industry."

**✓ After**
> "Your calendar fills itself — clients book online around the clock, and no-shows drop
> when the reminder goes out on time."

**Why:** Cut the tells ("innovative," "empowers," "seamlessly," "of all sizes," "in
today's fast-paced…"). Led with the owner's two concrete outcomes: bookings without
phone tag, fewer empty chairs.

**✗ Before (feature)**
> "The platform offers automated communication capabilities to facilitate the reduction
> of appointment no-shows."

**✓ After**
> "Every client gets an SMS the day before their visit — [X]% fewer no-shows for a
> typical salon."

**Why:** "offers … capabilities … to facilitate the reduction of" is wind-up; lead with
the mechanism and its timing. The stat stays a bracketed placeholder — proof is never
invented, in any domain.
